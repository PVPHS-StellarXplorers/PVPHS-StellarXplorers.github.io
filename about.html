About the Program

Palos Verdes Peninsula High School (PVPHS) provides the opportunity to compete in the Air & Space Forces Association’s (AFA) StellarXplorers Program.

Teams of 2-6 compete to solve realistic space scenarios – e.g. determining the optimal orbit to observe migrating whales, selecting the optimal satellite components to measure the moon’s water, determining the launch vehicle and system to launch a satellite to another planet, etc. – in a six-hour period. Teams are forced to trade choices in order to maximize their points in a multi-variable problem and compete against hundreds of other teams worldwide.



Competition Timeline

All teams compete in 6 rounds: 3 practice rounds and 3 qualifying rounds. The sum of their scores over the 3 qualifying rounds is used to determine their advancement to the semi-finals round, where their score is wiped. Their performance in the semi-final round determines their selection for the finals rounds, where the teams are flown out to compete with the rest of the top 10 teams.
- Round 1: Orbit selection round, using Ansys STK
- Round 2: Satellite components selection
- Round 3: Launch system selection
- Semi-finals: Combines rounds 1,2,&3 into an 8-hr competition
- Finals: Combines Semi-Finals with a presentation to the StellarXplorers board
